NHS VR Scenes
Made in Unity


Ten VR scenes were single-handedly crafted from scratch on a shoe-string budget, depicting scenarios created by the NHS. Many assets had to be sourced from TurboSquid and CGTrader, so generally were not designed to render efficiently in a game engine.

To remedy this, the OBJ-editor application written for my M.Sc. Final Project was re-used to combine separate objects that shared the same materials, dramatically reducing draw calls, and this was especially crucial for the highly-detailed bus model. Other pieces of bus geometry required separation and translation, in order to realistically animate the doors with correct hinge axes.




M.Sc. Final Project
Made in Unity


Architectural Building Information Modelling (BIM) data of Newcastle University's Urban Sciences Building was made available in the form of IFC files. These were converted into OBJ mesh files, resulting in over 22GB of geometry, 140,000 objects and 120 million vertices.

To process this data and make it "game engine-ready", a 10,000-line OBJ-editor application was written in C++. Selective mesh decimation and occlusion culling techniques were subsequently utilised to help maintain a smooth framerate in VR. Real-time sensor data from the building is streamed into the simulation via a WebAPI, allowing it to be visualised.
